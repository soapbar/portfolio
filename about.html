a little about moi

只有我們自己才可以決定自己的樣子: Only We Can Determine What We Are

"Hi! My name is Sophie."

is the first thing that comes out of my mouth at almost every occasion.
Without thinking, I identify myself as "Sophie", a name that represented a characteristic that my mother wanted me to have: wisdom. My legal name, Bin Ru (彬汝）, was given to me based on the day I was born by a name giver in China, with a meaning of politeness and consideration. After taking Korean in college, a close Korean friend gave me a Korean name, 소희, meaning bright. All the names that I associate with myself are what I identify as, and a representation of what I strive to be.

Trans: I was born in Shanghai in 1998. Even though I moved to Canada when I was two years old, I can still understand Shanghainese (the Shanghai dialect)! French is one of Quebec's official languages, so I spoke French in school until we moved to the United States when I was eight years old. In third grade, I came to the United States, which felt exactly the same as Canada, but yet completely different at the same time. In high school, I got introduced to Korean culture contents (K-pop, Korean drama) and it felt so new to me, yet I felt like I could relate to the content as well. In college I have been taking Korean language classes, and it's so fun! I realized that I enjoy learning new languages so I can communicate and empathize with people all around the world.
